A net-metered onsite solar PV system is one that is \u201cbehind-the-meter\u201d and directly-connected to a client\u2019s property.\u00a0 Commercial\/industrial systems typically range in size from 200 kW \u2013 2MW, but may be smaller or larger depending upon a client\u2019s property size and electricity consumption.\u00a0 System size is typically determined by the following:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • The client\u2019s electricity consumption, typically determined from the past 12-months of electric bills at the time of system analysis.<\/li>\n
  • The physical limits of the client\u2019s property in which to accommodate the solar PV system.\u00a0 In some cases, the system may be capable of generating more or less energy than the client consumes.<\/li>\n
  • Government regulations determining the use of backup storage and\/or overproduction of solar energy.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      \n

    • Property-attached ground array<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Develop a proposal based on system size and consumption for the following options:\u00a0<\/p>\n

          \n
        • PPA (a.k.a. Power Purchase Agreement) \u2013 a reduction in energy costs with no capital outlay to the client for installation or maintenance of the solar PV system.\u00a0 The client \u201chosts\u201d the system but does not receive any renewable incentives.\u00a0 There is also an option to purchase the system during the term of the contract.<\/li>\n
        • Solar loan\/cash purchase \u2013 The client can receive a federal tax credit and financial benefits, depending on the property and state incentives. \u00a0It also would receive free electricity for the life of the solar PV system (typically 25 years).<\/li>\n
        • Lease option \u2013 similar to a PPA, but the tax credits and other financial incentives belong to the owner of the system (not the client).<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
