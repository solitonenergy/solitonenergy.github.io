A utility-scale, grid-connected offsite solar PV system (a.k.a solar farm) is one that is \u201cin-front-of-the-meter\u201d and remote from the client\u2019s property.\u00a0 Such systems typically range in size from 2M-10MW, but can be much larger.\u00a0 System size is typically determined by the following:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • The physical size and cost of the land on which it is constructed.\u00a0 Approximately 4-5 acres per MW DC are required, plus additional land for setback and wetland considerations.<\/li>\n
  • The cost, complexity, and distance of the utility interconnection to the property is also of major concern.\u00a0 Larger solar farms may tolerate more expensive interconnects and substations in their business models, but smaller farms require cheap, simple ones.<\/li>\n
  • State incentives play a major role in the financial viability of a solar farm project.\u00a0 Without an offtaker (i.e. a large energy consumer to sign a PPA to use the energy output of the solar farm), the operator will typically have to accept wholesale pricing from the local utility, which is much cheaper.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Offsite solar is typically constructed (where permitted) on the following types of land:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Greenfields (land that is not farmland preserved, but may be farm assessed)<\/li>\n
    • Remediated brownfields and landfills<\/li>\n
    • Other types of land (e.g. rock quarries)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
